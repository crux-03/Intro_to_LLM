Decoding Strategies

Temperature, top-k, and top-p sampling — how to pick the next token from a probability distribution to control creativity and coherence.

Part 01

Greedy Decoding

The simplest strategy: always pick the most probable token. next = argmax(probs)

Deterministic and coherent, but boring and repetitive. Often gets stuck in loops.

▸ Greedy Always Picks the Same
Part 02

Temperature Sampling

Divide logits by temperature before softmax: probs = softmax(logits / T)

▸ Temperature Explorer
🧩 What does temperature = 0 converge to?

Part 03

Top-k Sampling

Only consider the k most likely tokens, zero out the rest, then sample. Prevents choosing wildly unlikely tokens.

top_k_logits, top_k_idx = torch.topk(logits, k)
logits = torch.full_like(logits, float('-inf'))
logits.scatter_(0, top_k_idx, top_k_logits)
probs = F.softmax(logits, dim=-1)
▸ Top-k Filtering
Part 04

Top-p (Nucleus) Sampling

Keep the smallest set of tokens whose cumulative probability ≥ p. Unlike top-k, this adapts to the distribution — more tokens when uncertain, fewer when confident.

▸ Top-p Cumulative Cutoff
📚 Top-p is like a VIP list: Only let in enough guests (tokens) to fill 90% of the venue. If one celebrity (high-prob token) takes up 80%, you only need 1-2 more. If the crowd is even, many get in. It adapts!

Part 05

Practical Guide

Greedy
Factual Q&A, code gen
T=0.3-0.7
Professional writing, summaries
T=0.7 + p=0.9
ChatGPT default! Conversation
T=1.0+
Brainstorming, poetry
ChatGPT uses temperature + top-p together. Temperature controls the overall sharpness, then top-p clips the long tail. This gives both coherence and variety.
