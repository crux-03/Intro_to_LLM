Part 01

The Sentiment Classification Task

Sentiment analysis is one of the most common NLP tasks. Given a piece of text, the model predicts its emotional tone — in our case, positive or negative.

"This movie was amazing!"       → Positive  (class 1)
"Terrible waste of time."       → Negative  (class 0)
"A masterpiece of cinema!"      → Positive  (class 1)
"I couldn't wait for it to end." → Negative  (class 0)

Part 03

Evaluation Metrics

For classification, we care about much more than just accuracy. Different metrics tell us different things about model quality.

The Confusion Matrix

Everything starts with the confusion matrix — a 2x2 table of prediction outcomes:

▸ Interactive Confusion Matrix

Adjust the values to see how metrics change:

Accuracy
(TP+TN) / Total. Overall correct rate. Can be misleading with imbalanced data!
Precision
TP / (TP+FP). "When we predict positive, how often are we right?"
Recall
TP / (TP+FN). "Of all actual positives, how many did we catch?"
F1 Score
Harmonic mean of precision and recall. Best single metric for balanced evaluation.
Why accuracy isn't enough: If 95% of emails are not spam, a model that always predicts "not spam" gets 95% accuracy but catches zero spam! Precision and recall reveal this failure. Always look at multiple metrics.

Part 04

Making Predictions

Once trained, making a prediction is straightforward: tokenize the input, run a forward pass, and take the argmax of the output logits.

def predict_sentiment(model, tokenizer, text):
    model.eval()
    input_ids = torch.tensor([tokenizer.encode(text, max_length=32)])

    with torch.no_grad():
        logits, _ = model(input_ids)
        probs = F.softmax(logits, dim=-1)
        pred = logits.argmax(1).item()

    sentiment = "Positive" if pred == 1 else "Negative"
    confidence = probs[0, pred].item()
    return sentiment, confidence
